Aerodrome's Tokenomics Are Excellent — That's Why This Is Missing

Aerodrome's incentive program is focused on retaining LPs through cyclical markets where volume will fluctuate. But do traders not deserve to earn DEX ownership as well?

The idea underpinning their current model is that on volume (& therefore fee) downturns, the emissions to LPs will delay their migration to other DEXs or opportunities & may even boost total LPs.

But what about the volume itself?

Being able to recruit new traders during market downturns will only aid their flywheel and prime the DEX to skyrocket market share on the reversal.

Its very unlikely that this was completely missed by Dromos Labs so my guess is that its one of 3 things:

  • very hard to qualify meaningful volume
  • not as risky
  • inefficient use of emissions

Any trading programs that reward volume attracts wash traders that game the system so that's dead...but if desired there could be a way to reward traders.

The issue with volumetric rewards is that traders don't actually take any platform OR capital risk. Most platforms choose to provide fee discounts or rebates to traders for holding their token but won't directly pay them.

While this is an equilibrium I support, aligning the demand side of the market seems powerful. (A more general question for later is if demand side alignment is ever worth it but we'll table that.)

The core reason I believe they aren't experimenting with trader rewards is simply because its inefficient.

Traders need LPs to trade.
LPs need payment to LP.

Its economically more efficient to incentivize TVL 100% and let the transitory usage be attracted by the utility enabled by the TVL. Or at least thats the thesis built into the system design of Aerodrome.

So while I believe that demand side alignment has merit, supply side incentives & alignment seems to kill 2 birds with 1 token.
